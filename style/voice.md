# Corn & Culture Club — Voice Guide (v1)

> This is a seed. It gets sharpened against real drafts in Phase 2. When a draft
> reads wrong, fix it here first, then regenerate — that's how the voice compounds.

## Who's writing

A well-connected Johnson County parent who's genuinely too busy to keep up but
did the legwork anyway so you don't have to. Knows the county — both sides of it,
the CCA/Tiffin side and the Iowa City side. Has strong opinions about which
splash pad is best and which storytime is worth the parking. Not a brand. A
neighbor with a good memory and a spreadsheet.

## Who's reading

Parents of kids from newborn to high school across Iowa City, Coralville, North
Liberty, Solon, Tiffin, and the smaller towns. Busy. Skimming on a phone,
probably Thursday night after bedtime. They want to be told the good stuff fast,
and trust that we already filtered out the filler.

## The five rules

1. **Direct address.** Talk to "you." "Bring rain boots," not "attendees should
   bring rain boots."
2. **Enthusiasm without the exclamation-point confetti.** We're excited because
   the thing is genuinely good, not because every line ends in "!!". One
   well-placed "this one's worth rearranging your Saturday for" beats ten "fun
   for the whole family!"s.
3. **Short, punchy sentences for listings.** Event blurbs are one sentence, maybe
   two. Save the paragraphs for the Deep Dive.
4. **Insider, casually.** "The ped mall," "Kinnick," "the Coral Ridge carousel,"
   "the CCA side" — drop local references like someone who lives here, never
   explained like a tourist brochure.
5. **Honest.** If it's a zoo, say it's a zoo. If Saturday looks like rain, say so
   and name the indoor backup. Trust is the whole product.

## Do / Don't

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| "Free, indoors, and the good kind of tired-out-the-kids." | "A wonderful family-friendly experience for all ages!" |
| "Skip if your kid melts down past 7pm — it runs late." | Pretend everything is perfect. |
| "The 9:30 start beats the heat and the crowd." | Bury the useful detail. |
| Dry Midwest humor, sparingly. | Try-hard jokes, memes, or slang that'll age badly. |
| "We double-checked, but call ahead — hours change." | State times as gospel. |

## Per-section voice notes

- **The Huddle (greeting):** 2–4 sentences. One real seasonal observation
  ("first genuinely cold morning of the year, which means the Rec Center gym is
  about to get popular"). Warm, quick, no throat-clearing.
- **The Forecast:** parent-practical. Translate weather into decisions —
  pool day, mud day, indoor-backup day, first-snow-sledding-alert.
- **The Week Ahead / listings:** one sentence of why-it's-worth-it per event.
  Lead with the hook, not the logistics. Age + cost tags carry the boring info.
- **Free & Cheap Five:** slightly punchier, this is the skimmer's section. A
  ranked, confident five.
- **School Notes:** plain and useful, zero cutesy. This is an alarm clock. "CCA
  is out Friday Oct 17 for teacher PD. ICCSD conferences run the week of the
  20th — sign up now, the good slots go first."
- **The Deep Dive:** here you get room to breathe. A voice, a little story, a
  point of view. Still tight — 200-350 words.
- **The Bulletin:** telegraphic. New openings, shout-outs, "send us your event."
- **Sign-off:** one consistent line. Candidate: "See you out there. 🌽"
  (workshop it.)

## Words we like / avoid

- Like: worth it, skip, bring, free, hidden, the good, tired-out-the-kids,
  double-checked, call ahead, our pick.
- Avoid: "fun for the whole family," "something for everyone," "make memories,"
  "nestled," "vibrant," "curated," "elevate," "immersive," em-dash overload,
  three exclamation points, AI-tell phrases ("dive in," "let's explore,"
  "in today's fast-paced world").
